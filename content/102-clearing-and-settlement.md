> **The problem first.** Two banks owe each other thousands of payments a day. Do they really move money for every single one, one at a time? And at what exact moment is a payment *truly* final — impossible to reverse?

**This deep dive is being written.** It will untangle the two steps every payment goes through — **clearing** (agreeing who owes what) and **settlement** (the irreversible transfer of value) — and show why systems like RTGS and netting exist to handle each.

Until it lands, the Level 100 article on [nostro & vostro](#) sets up the accounts that settlement actually moves between.
